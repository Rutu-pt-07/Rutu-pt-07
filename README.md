### Hey, I'm Rutu 👋

</div>

I'm an AI & ML researcher obsessed with one question: **what does it take for a machine to truly understand its world?**

My work sits at the crossroads of **cognitive architectures, robotics, and healthcare AI** — building systems that don't just process data, but *reason*, *adapt*, and *act* in the real world. I believe the best AI doesn't replace human intelligence — it amplifies it.

> *"The goal is not to build a machine that thinks like a human. It's to build one that thinks alongside one."*

## 🧠 What I Actually Do

I design and build **intelligent systems** that combine:

| Layer | What It Means |
|-------|--------------|
| 👁️ **Perceive** | Computer vision, sensor fusion, making sense of messy real-world data |
| 🧩 **Reason** | Contextual decision-making, cognitive architectures, knowledge representation |
| 📚 **Learn** | Deep learning, reinforcement learning, adaptive models |
| 🤖 **Act** | Robotic control, autonomous planning, human-in-the-loop systems |

Primary application domains: **Healthcare AI** and **Autonomous Robotics**

## 🚀 Selected Projects

### 🏥 Healthcare Diagnostic Intelligence
> ML/DL models for medical data analysis and intelligent diagnostic decision support

Building predictive systems that surface patterns in clinical data — the kind of signal that's invisible to the naked eye but critical for early intervention. Focus on interpretability, because black-box models have no place in medicine.

`PyTorch` `Scikit-learn` `Medical Imaging` `Explainable AI`

---

### 👁️ Vision-Based Perception for Robotics
> Deep learning architectures for image classification and robot perception

Developing perception pipelines that give robots spatial awareness — the ability to identify, locate, and respond to objects in unstructured environments. Because a robot that can't see, can't act.

`OpenCV` `CNNs` `ROS2` `Sensor Fusion`

## 🧭 Research Philosophy

I don't build AI to automate people out of jobs.

I build AI to handle the parts of a job that drain human potential — the repetitive, the error-prone, the data-heavy — so humans can focus on the parts that actually need humanity: **empathy, creativity, judgment, and care.**

That's the version of AI worth building.
